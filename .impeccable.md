# Design Context — Arpit Garg Portfolio

## Users
Visitors are research collaborators (CVPR/NeurIPS/TPAMI/ECCV reviewers and authors), industry recruiters (FAANG and frontier-AI labs), VFX studio producers, and journalists looking to cover the work. They arrive on a laptop or desktop in a quiet evening browsing session — the site is consumed slowly, not in a frantic mobile context. They need to understand within 30 seconds: "this is a senior ML researcher who ships." Most will scan, a few will read deeply.

## Brand Personality
**Three words: rigorous, cinematic, considered.**

The site should feel like a Criterion Collection booklet for a research career — sober, confident, premium without theatrics. It must read as senior, not student; published, not aspirational. Voice is first-person plural in spirit ("research that ships") rather than gushy self-promotion. Tone errs toward understatement: the work earns the volume, not the design.

Emotional target: confidence and quiet authority. Visitors should leave thinking "this person is the real thing."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Theme — dark, warm.** Deep warm-black background (tinted toward the gold accent hue, not true #000), warm bone/paper foreground (not pure white), single gold accent used sparingly. Cinematic without being glowy or neon.

**References (chosen):**
- Premium design studios — asymmetric layouts, distinctive typography, motion as polish, restraint over decoration.
- Magazine / publication — NYT projects, The Pudding, ProPublica. Editorial type at scale, scroll-driven moments, photography that breathes.

**Anti-references:**
- Generic agency dark-mode portfolios with cyan-on-black, purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism, sparkle particles.
- Templated personal-research sites built from defaults (Inter + Source Sans + plain markdown).
- Designy-but-empty: motion for motion's sake, gradient text, hero metric blocks.

**Typography — committed.**
- Display: **Erode** (Indian Type Foundry, free via Fontshare CDN). Variable, high-contrast editorial serif. Looks like a film title card; pairs with a confident italic. Same aesthetic register as PP Editorial New but genuinely free for commercial use.
- Body: **Switzer** (ITF, free via Fontshare CDN). Variable neo-grotesque, refined enough for editorial body, warm enough not to feel corporate.
- Mono: **JetBrains Mono** — neutral, technical, restrained.

Avoided defaults: Inter, Fraunces, Instrument Serif, IBM Plex, DM Sans, Lora, Cormorant, Playfair (all are AI-reflex picks). Considered and rejected: PP Editorial New (paid, $40+).

**Color — committed.**
- Background: warm near-black `oklch(0.16 0.005 70)` — tinted toward the gold hue (~70°), not true black.
- Foreground: warm bone `oklch(0.95 0.005 70)` — not pure white.
- Mid-tones: warm grays tinted toward the same 70° hue for tonal cohesion.
- Accent: refined gold `oklch(0.75 0.12 75)` — single sharp accent, used at ~10% visual weight max (60-30-10 rule).
- All neutrals tinted toward the gold hue creates subconscious cohesion.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the design.** Every element exists to make the work itself read more clearly. No ornament that doesn't earn its place.
2. **Restraint amplifies impact.** A single gold underline, an italic display headline, one cinematic image — each lands because nothing around it competes.
3. **Editorial first, interactive second.** This is a magazine you scroll, not a product you operate. Motion serves reading; it doesn't replace it.
4. **Hierarchy through contrast, not size alone.** Weight + color + space + size together. Light type on dark needs more leading, more contrast, more breathing room.
5. **No AI defaults.** Pure black, pure white, gradient text, Inter, glow-on-dark accents, sparkle particles, hero metric blocks, side-stripe borders — all banned.
